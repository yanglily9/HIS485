In conclusion, marriage advertisements was seemingly a method that granted women more choices and freedom to be selective by listing requirements for their potential partners. The limitation of this research was that there were limited resources that can be allocated from on Shen Bao, Chinese Periodical Full-text Database (1911-1949), and Da Cheng Fulltext Database of Old Journals. After collecting 161 marriage advertisement between 1920 to 1930, Gao Daohang (高岛航) drew the conclusion that marriage advertisement not only resembles the ideals of the modern notion of marriage, but also contributes to the completion of the traditional marriage. This again demonstrated that the new method allowed the selective structure of marriage advertisements; yet, women were still stagnant in their social standing because they had to present themselves in a way that was appealing to men.
