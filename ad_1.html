This marriage advertisement was published on August 15, 1946. [1] She is from Shandong, age twenty-three. She went to a women’s university but dropped out for three years. She is beautiful (品貌秀麗) and healthy. She can live independently. She lives in a “liberation district” after her parents and brothers were killed by the labour party. She is living with her friends temporarily and she has an office job. She is looking for a male companion: 1) he has to have the awareness about his own nation and culture 2) he has to have the determination to seek revenge for her parents. 3) he needs to be between twenty-five and thirty five (年在二十五至三十五歲者) 4) He needs to be a university graduate (大學畢業）and have skills or attend to military school. 5) he needs to be healthy (體格健康) 6) His family needs to have longterm capital assets (家有恆產) and his parents must be friendly. If any male fits the listed six requirements, he should prepare a recently picture of himself and a letter. Mail it to the newspaper press for Miss Kong. If the requirement does not match, it will be send back.

Key point: Miss Kong’s initiation to post this advertisement demonstrated that she had the ability and freedom to choose her own partner instead of following the traditional arranged marriage. Her requirements for her potential partner reflected that she could formulate her own expectations for her partner, as it is no longer only men who could create expectations for women. Additionally, publishing on a public press demonstrated that she felt comfortable to express herself. She held high expectations for her potential partner: wealthy, educated, and healthy.
